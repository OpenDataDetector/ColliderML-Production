Daniel is an HEP physicist working on the ColliderML project — an open ML benchmark for high-energy physics detector data. He works at NERSC (Perlmutter). The project has two repos: `colliderml_lib` (public library) and `colliderml_dev` (production pipeline). He prefers energy-weighted metrics over count-based, algorithm-agnostic evaluation, and thinks about clustering from first principles (physics-motivated, not just ML metrics). He's interested in CMS/FCC-style high-granularity calorimeter approaches rather than ATLAS topo-clustering.
